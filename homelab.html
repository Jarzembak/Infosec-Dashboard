Home Lab Resources

A home lab is essential for a cyber security professional (or enthusiast). There are so many tutorials available for free (or at a low cost), that it makes it extremely easy to get stuck in the “learning” phase, without actually getting your hands on anything. Combine that with the plethora of certifications marketed towards job seekers, and you have a recipe for what is commonly referred to as “tutorial hell”. Your home lab is where real learning and understanding happens, and it is important to invest time in it. Even if you don't have high end hardware, there are still ways you can experiment at home.

Virtualization

The first thing I would recommend is some way to run virtual machines or containers. A virtual machine is an operating system that runs virtually within the operating system running on your computer. This virtual machine runs within a hypervisor, which sort of tricks it into thinking it is a totally separate computer. They can be completely isolated from your other software and apps, and can be created and deleted quickly, so if something goes wrong, you can easily start over without any consequences. Containers are similar, but instead of an entire operating system, you have a single app, or group of apps. These are often used by security researchers to package up a piece of vulnerable software with its configurations and dependencies all kept within the container. These can also be isolated from the rest of your system. Here is a list of common hypervisors and container management software, which you will need for virtualization.
